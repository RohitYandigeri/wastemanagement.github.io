Project Details

This project focuses on developing a sustainable, data-driven waste management system aimed at creating a cleaner, more efficient, and reliable smart city. The system incorporates innovative technologies and strategies for waste collection, segregation, recycling, and disposal to ensure the city’s sustainability.

  • Smart Waste Collection:
    • Use of smart bins with IoT sensors to monitor fill levels and optimize waste collection routes.
    • Dynamic scheduling of waste collection based on real-time data from bins, reducing fuel consumption and emissions.
    • Alert system for overflow, notifying residents or management systems when bins are nearing capacity.
  • Waste Segregation and Recycling:
    • Designated color-coded zones for clear segregation of different waste types (e.g., biodegradable, plastic, e-waste).
    • Recycling hubs for residents to drop off recyclables or large items such as electronics.
  • Underground Waste Disposal:
    • Underground bins embedded in high-density areas to save space and keep streets clean.
    • Pneumatic waste disposal systems that transport waste via underground pipes to centralized facilities.
  • Community Composting and Biogas Plants:
    • Urban composting stations located in residential or park zones to process organic waste.
    • Biogas plants near food markets or restaurants to convert organic waste into energy.
  • Digital Waste Management Platforms:
    • Resident apps to report issues, request pickups, or track waste segregation scores.
    • Analytics dashboards for city authorities to monitor waste collection performance and recycling rates.
  • Hazardous Waste Management:
    • Specialized zones for the disposal of hazardous materials, such as batteries or medical waste, with appropriate safety measures.
    • Periodic drives to collect hazardous items like e-waste, paints, or chemicals from residents.
